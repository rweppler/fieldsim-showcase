02 — showdown doctrine

What the single-game engine knows

Every rule below is measured, not vibes — burned in from calibration passes that made the boards worse before they made them better.

D-01Captain odds are tail data

The feed's CPT Optimal% is the probability a player captains the salary-best lineup. FieldSim tunes each player's tail weight until its own sims reproduce that ladder — the market's captain odds become the distribution's tails.

D-02Hot hands get more skew

At fixed mean and variance, raising tail weight lowers the p85–p95 region that decides optimal captaincy. TD-or-bust punts get more skew; volume alphas get less. Two earlier tunings assumed the opposite and made every board worse.

D-03Six-man builds herd hard

Punt captains stud their five flex spots — that's the whole point of punting the slot. The field generator herds those templates near-greedily, so "unique" cheap captains duplicate six ways, not 1.4.

D-04Dupes and win odds are one draw

A construction's duplication rate is measured from the generator's own process — the same distribution that sets its win odds. Pricing them separately is how punt DSTs end up faking 1,000% ROI.

D-05Budgets, not pairs

A per-sim scoring budget with lumpy within-team shares drives the rank dependence — six near-independent medians can't quietly stack into a winning cheap-captain build.

D-06Both ladders, always

CPT and FLEX are tracked as different assets everywhere — two ownership ladders, slot-aware exposures, and exports headed CPT,FLEX,FLEX,FLEX,FLEX,FLEX.
