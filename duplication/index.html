01 — the three laws of the window

Duplication is priced, never punished

The model isn't a penalty slider someone tuned to feel right. It's bookkeeping — three rules that keep ten thousand entries' worth of prizes exactly conserved.

LAW IThe size-biased window

If a construction's expected copies per field is λ, the count given you booked it is λ/(1−e^−λ) — you can't observe your own lineup zero times. Chalk compounds: popular tickets are popular precisely when they're printed.

LAW IIThe floor at one

Copies never drop below 1.0. A unique lineup keeps its whole prize — and that is all it gets. Sub-one multipliers were tried, flattered longshots, and were removed. Uniqueness is a shield, not a sword.

LAW IIIConservation of rake

After every ledge is split, the payout curve is repriced to N_eff = Σ max(1, λᵢ) effective entries, so the entry-weighted mean ROI of the whole field is −rake to a rounding error. The house always balances; the only question is who pays whom.
