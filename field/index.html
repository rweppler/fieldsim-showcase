01 — exposure fidelity

The loom counts its threads

An imagined field is worthless if its ownership drifts from the slate. Each bar below is one player's woven exposure; the notch is his projected ownership. The generator holds every thread inside a hard tolerance band — within two points absolute and fifteen percent relative, whichever is looser — and players under 1% are capped at 1%.

This isn't a soft calibration that hopes for the best. Scarce positions are filled first — the warp is strung before the shuttle flies — so the salary squeeze lands on deep receiver pools, not on the two viable tight ends. Forced overflow spreads to the least-used thread, never onto the chalk.

02 — loom rules

How a crowd actually drafts

The field generator is not a random-lineup machine. It is a model of ten thousand humans with the same projections, the same salary cap, and the same weaknesses — herding onto the same stacks, doubling the same chalk, and abandoning the same fragile punts. The weave has rules, and the rules are the realism.

i

Ownership is gravity. Thread selection is weighted by projected ownership directly — never normalized. A correct slate already sums to roster capacity.

ii

Warp before weft. DST and TE are strung first; the deep WR pool absorbs the salary squeeze. Reorder this and the whole cloth distorts.

iii

Hard caps, not hopes. Every player's exposure wears a ceiling of projection + tolerance. Overflow spreads to the least-used thread.

iv

The cloth is the contest. These ten thousand rows are the opponents every candidate lineup must out-place, Sunday after simulated Sunday.
